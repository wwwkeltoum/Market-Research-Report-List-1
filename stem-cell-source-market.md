<p><p>Stem cells are undifferentiated cells that have the potential to differentiate into various specialized cell types in the body. They have gained significant attention in the medical field due to their unique regenerative capabilities, making them promising for treating various diseases and injuries. The source of these stem cells plays a crucial role in their usability and effectiveness.</p><p>There are two primary sources of stem cells: embryonic and adult (including both somatic and induced pluripotent stem cells). Embryonic stem cells are derived from embryos, while adult stem cells are obtained from different tissues of the body. The choice of stem cell source depends on various factors such as the intended application, availability, ethical considerations, and regulatory restrictions.</p><p>The stem cell source market has witnessed considerable growth in recent years and is expected to continue expanding in the coming years.
