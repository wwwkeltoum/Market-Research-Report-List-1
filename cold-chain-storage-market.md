<p><ul><li>Chilled</li><li>Frozen</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cold chain storage is a specialized market that caters to the preservation and distribution of temperature-sensitive products. The market can be broadly divided into two types: chilled and frozen. Chilled storage refers to the storage of perishable items, such as fruits, vegetables, and dairy products, at temperatures above freezing but below room temperature. Frozen storage, on the other hand, involves maintaining products at temperatures below freezing, typically used for long-term storage of items like meat, fish, and ice cream. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring the quality and safety of these products throughout the supply chain.</p></p>
