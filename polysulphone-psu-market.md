<p><p>Polysulfone (PSU) is a high-performance thermoplastic polymer that offers excellent mechanical, thermal, and electrical properties. It is commonly used in various industries, such as automotive, aerospace, electronics, and medical. The PSU market can be categorized into three segments: Polysulfone (PSF), Polyarylsulfone, and Polyethersulfone. PSF is a type of PSU derived from bisphenol A, offering high toughness and chemical resistance. Polyarylsulfone is a modified PSU that provides improved dimensional stability and flame resistance. Polyethersulfone, on the other hand, offers superior hydrolytic stability and is often used in water treatment and filtration applications.</p></p>
