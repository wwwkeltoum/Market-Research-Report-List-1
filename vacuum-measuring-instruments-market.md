<p><p>Vacuum measuring instruments are categorized into three main types: low vacuum, medium vacuum, and high vacuum. Low vacuum instruments are capable of measuring pressure levels from atmospheric pressure down to around 10^-3 Torr. Medium vacuum instruments are used to measure pressure levels ranging from 10^-3 to 10^-8 Torr. High vacuum instruments, on the other hand, can measure pressure levels below 10^-8 Torr, providing precise measurements for applications requiring extremely low pressure environments. Each vacuum type serves varied purposes across industries like research, manufacturing, and scientific applications.</p></p>
