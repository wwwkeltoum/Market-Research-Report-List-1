<p><p>Alpha thalassemia is a genetic blood disorder characterized by insufficient production of alpha globin chains, leading to anemia. The market for treating alpha thalassemia includes two major types, namely Iron Chelating Drugs and Gene Therapy. Iron Chelating Drugs help remove excess iron from the body, which accumulates due to frequent blood transfusions required in alpha thalassemia. On the other hand, Gene Therapy aims to correct the underlying genetic defect by introducing functional copies of the alpha globin gene into the patient's cells. Both approaches play important roles in managing alpha thalassemia.</p></p>
