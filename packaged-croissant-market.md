<p><p>Packaged croissant market types include shelf-stable croissants and frozen croissants. Shelf-stable croissants refer to those that can be stored at room temperature without refrigeration. They are often packaged in airtight containers to maintain their freshness for extended periods. On the other hand, frozen croissants are pre-made croissants that are frozen and sold in packaging. They need to be stored in a freezer and require thawing and baking before consumption. Both options provide convenience and offer a longer shelf life compared to freshly baked croissants.</p></p>
