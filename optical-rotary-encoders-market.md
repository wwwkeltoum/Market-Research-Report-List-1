<p><p>Optical rotary encoders are devices used to measure the rotational angle and position of objects. These encoders consist of a rotary disk with evenly distributed transparent and opaque sections that spin in front of a light source and a detector. As the disk rotates, the light beams passing through the transparent sections are detected by the photodetector, generating electrical signals that can be converted into rotational data.</p><p>The optical rotary encoders market has been witnessing significant growth in recent years. The increasing demand for automation and precision control systems across various industries such as automotive, aerospace, electronics, and healthcare is driving the market growth. Optical rotary encoders are extensively used in applications such as motor control, robotics, packaging machines, and CNC machines to provide accurate position and speed information.</p><p>Moreover, the growing adoption of optical rotary encoders in advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and electric vehicles is further fueling the market growth. <p><strong>The Optical Rotary Encoders Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Incremental Encoders</li><li>Absolute Encoders</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Optical rotary encoders are devices used to measure rotational movement in various applications. Incremental encoders generate a series of pulses as the encoder shaft rotates, allowing for relative position measurement. They are commonly used in motion control systems. On the other hand, absolute encoders provide a unique digital code for each position, enabling precise absolute positioning without the need for a reference point. They find applications in highly accurate positioning systems where accuracy and reliability are crucial. Both types play a significant role in different industries, meeting diverse requirements for rotational movement measurement.</p></p>
