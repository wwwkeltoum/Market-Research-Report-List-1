<p><p>Hydrogen fuel cells are of two main types: air-cooled and transport-cooled. Air-cooled fuel cells use air to cool the cell during operation, making them suitable for small-scale applications such as portable power devices and backup power systems. On the other hand, transport-cooled fuel cells utilize a coolant to maintain optimal operating temperatures, suiting them for larger applications like vehicles and industrial power systems. Both types of fuel cells offer a sustainable and efficient alternative to traditional combustion-based technologies by converting hydrogen and oxygen into electricity, with water as the only byproduct.</p></p>
