<p><p>Vacuum-insulated switchgears are categorized into two types: primary switchgear and secondary switchgear. Primary switchgear is installed in high voltage power systems to control and protect electrical equipment. It includes components like circuit breakers, disconnectors, and current transformers. Secondary switchgear, on the other hand, is used in low voltage power systems to distribute and control electricity within buildings or industrial facilities. It includes components such as distribution boards, busbars, and switch disconnectors. Both primary and secondary switchgear play a crucial role in ensuring safe and efficient electrical power distribution.</p></p>
