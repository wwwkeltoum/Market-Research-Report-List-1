<p><p>The Deep Reactive Ion Etching (DRIE) System market consists of two prominent types: Bosch Process and Cryo Process. The Bosch Process employs a combination of alternating etching and passivation steps, resulting in high aspect ratio structures with vertical sidewalls. This method is commonly used for manufacturing MEMS devices and 3D structures. On the other hand, the Cryo Process involves cooling the substrate to low temperatures during the etching process, yielding superior etch profiles with minimal sidewall scalloping. This technique is advantageous for applications requiring high precision and smooth surfaces.</p></p>
