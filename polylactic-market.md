<p><p>The polylactic market consists of three main types: Poly(L-lactic)acid, Poly(D-lactic)acid, and Poly(DL-lactic)acid. Poly(L-lactic)acid is made from the polymerization of L-lactic acid, which is derived from renewable resources. It is known for its biodegradable and biocompatible properties, making it suitable for various applications in the medical and packaging industries. Poly(D-lactic)acid is made from D-lactic acid and has similar properties to Poly(L-lactic)acid. Poly(DL-lactic)acid is a racemic mixture of L-lactic and D-lactic acids, resulting in a polymer with intermediate properties and wider applications.</p></p>
