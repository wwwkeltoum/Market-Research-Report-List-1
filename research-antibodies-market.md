<p><p>The research antibodies market consists of two main types: monoclonal antibodies (mAbs) and polyclonal antibodies (pAbs). Monoclonal antibodies are produced from a single clone of B cells and specifically target a particular protein, while polyclonal antibodies are generated from multiple B cell clones and recognize multiple epitopes on a target protein. mAbs are highly specific and widely used in research and therapeutic applications, while pAbs are more diverse and may provide broader targeting capabilities. Both types play crucial roles in advancing scientific knowledge and medical breakthroughs.</p></p>
