<p><p>Carbon anode materials in the market include various types such as natural graphite, artificial graphite, mesocarbon microspheres, and graphene. Natural graphite is mined and processed from graphite ore, while artificial graphite is produced through high-temperature treatment of carbon sources. Mesocarbon microspheres are synthetic carbon spheres with high purity and uniformity, ideal for battery applications. Graphene, a two-dimensional carbon allotrope, offers exceptional electrical conductivity and mechanical strength. Each type of carbon anode material offers unique properties and benefits for various applications.</p></p>
