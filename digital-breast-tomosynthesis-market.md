<p><p>Digital Breast Tomosynthesis (DBT) is an advanced imaging technology used for the early detection and diagnosis of breast cancer. It is an extension of digital mammography that captures a series of low-dose x-ray images of the breast from different angles. These images are then reconstructed into a three-dimensional (3D) representation, providing a clear and comprehensive view of breast tissue. DBT offers higher sensitivity and specificity compared to traditional mammography, as it helps eliminate overlapping structures and improves the visualization of abnormal tissue.</p><p>The global Digital Breast Tomosynthesis Market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>Digital Breast Tomosynthesis (DBT) market consists of two types: Stand-alone DBT equipment and Integrated market. </p><p>Stand-alone DBT equipment refers to individual machines specifically designed for breast tomosynthesis imaging. These machines are typically used in dedicated breast imaging centers or hospitals. They offer 3D imaging capabilities, allowing radiologists to capture multiple images of the breast from different angles, resulting in improved detection and characterization of breast lesions.</p><p>Integrated market, on the other hand, involves the integration of DBT capabilities into existing mammography systems. This integration enables healthcare facilities to upgrade their mammography units without the need for separate DBT equipment. Such integration offers convenience and cost-efficiency by allowing 2D and 3D imaging to be performed on the same machine, reducing the need for additional equipment and space.</p></p>
