<p><p>The non-contact steering torque sensor market includes two major types: electric power steering sensor and magnetic sensing steering sensor. Electric power steering sensors use electrical signals to measure the torque applied to the steering wheel, providing accurate and real-time data for the control of power steering systems. On the other hand, magnetic sensing steering sensors utilize magnetic field changes to measure the torque applied, ensuring precise and reliable steering control. Both types of sensors play a crucial role in enhancing the safety and performance of vehicles by providing accurate torque measurements.</p></p>
