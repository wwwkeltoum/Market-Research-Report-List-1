<p><p>A doorbell is a device typically installed at the entrance of a property that signals the arrival of visitors through a sound or visual indication. Firstly, the growing trend of smart homes and increasing integration of home automation systems have significantly boosted the demand for smart doorbells. The convenience and enhanced security offered by smart doorbells have made them increasingly sought after.</p><p>Moreover, the rising emphasis on home security, driven by factors such as increasing crime rates and the desire for enhanced privacy, has further fueled the demand for doorbells with advanced security features. <p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The doorbell market comprises three types of doorbells: wired, wireless visible, and wireless invisible doorbells. Wired doorbells are connected to the building's electrical system, requiring professional installation but ensuring a reliable and constant power source. Wireless visible doorbells utilize radio frequency to transmit signals between the button and receiver, providing flexibility for installation and often including video capabilities. On the other hand, wireless invisible doorbells utilize motion sensors instead of a physical button, alerting homeowners when someone approaches the door without the need for a visible device.</p></p>
