<p><p>Poultry breeding system refers to the process of selectively breeding chickens, turkeys, ducks, and other poultry species to improve their genetic characteristics and overall productivity. It involves careful selection of parent birds based on desired traits such as growth rate, meat quality, egg production, disease resistance, and feed efficiency. The aim of poultry breeding is to produce offspring with desirable traits, which are then used for commercial meat or egg production.</p><p>In recent years, the demand for poultry products has been increasing rapidly due to the growing population and changing dietary preferences globally. Genomic selection involves the use of DNA testing to identify favorable genetic traits in poultry and select parent birds accordingly. This technique allows breeders to enhance the genetic potential of poultry populations more accurately and efficiently.</p><p>Another trend in the market is the emphasis on animal welfare and sustainability. Consumers are increasingly concerned about the conditions in which poultry is raised and the environmental impact of poultry production.
