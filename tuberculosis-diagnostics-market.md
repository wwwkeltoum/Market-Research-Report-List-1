<p><p>The tuberculosis diagnostics market consists of various types, including culture-based, sputum smear microscopy, rapid molecular, and others. Culture-based diagnostics involve growing the tuberculosis bacteria in a laboratory setting to identify it. Sputum smear microscopy is a simple and inexpensive method used to detect tuberculosis by examining sputum samples under a microscope. Rapid molecular diagnostics use genetic testing techniques to quickly identify the tuberculosis bacteria in a patient's sample. The "others" category comprises newer and evolving diagnostic methods, such as nucleic acid amplification tests and line probe assays, that offer faster and more accurate results.</p></p>
