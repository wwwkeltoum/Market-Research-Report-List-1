<p><p>Hydronic underfloor heating is a type of radiant heating system that uses water to transfer heat from a source (such as a boiler) to pipes installed underneath the flooring. These pipes distribute the heat evenly throughout the space, providing comfortable and efficient heating.</p><p>The main components of a hydronic underfloor heating system include piping, manifolds, pumps, thermostats, valves, and controls. These components work together to create a closed-loop system that constantly circulates hot water through the pipes, maintaining a consistent temperature in the room.</p><p>The hydronic underfloor heating component market is experiencing steady growth due to factors such as increasing demand for energy-efficient heating systems, rising awareness about the benefits of underfloor heating, and growing construction activities across the globe. The integration of smart technology enhances the convenience and comfort of using hydronic underfloor heating systems.</p><p>Another trend in the market is the growing adoption of renewable energy sources in hydronic underfloor heating systems.
