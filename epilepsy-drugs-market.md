<p><p>Epilepsy drugs can be classified into two generations: first generation and second generation. First generation drugs include medications like phenobarbital and valproic acid, which have been in use for a longer time and are more commonly prescribed. They work by targeting specific receptors in the brain to reduce seizures. Second generation drugs, such as lamotrigine and levetiracetam, are newer and have been developed to be more selective and have fewer side effects. They also target various mechanisms involved in seizures to provide better control over epilepsy.</p></p>
