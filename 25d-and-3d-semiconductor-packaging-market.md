<p><p>2.5D and 3D semiconductor packaging refer to advanced techniques used in the assembly of electronic components. In 2.5D packaging, multiple chips are stacked together using through-silicon vias (TSVs) to create a compact and efficient structure. 3D packaging encompasses various methods like wire bonding, TSV, and fan-out technologies. Wire bonding involves connecting chips using tiny wires, while TSV enables vertical connections through drilled holes. Fan-out technology redistributes the connections from the chip to a larger substrate, allowing for increased functionality. These packaging methods enhance performance, reduce size, and improve efficiency in electronic devices.</p></p>
