<p><strong>The Vascular Patches Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Biologic Vascular Patches</li><li>Synthetic Vascular Patches</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Vascular patches are used during surgeries to repair or replace damaged or diseased blood vessels. There are two main types of vascular patches: biologic vascular patches and synthetic vascular patches. Biologic vascular patches are made from animal tissues, such as bovine pericardium or human umbilical veins, which are treated to remove cells and preserve the extracellular matrix. Synthetic vascular patches, on the other hand, are made from materials like expanded polytetrafluoroethylene (ePTFE) or polyester. Both types of patches have different advantages and applications, and their selection depends on the specific needs and preferences of surgeons and patients.</p></p>

Vascular patches are used in these procedures to repair or reinforce blood vessels, ensuring proper blood circulation.</p></p>
