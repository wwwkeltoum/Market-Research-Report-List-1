<p><p>PM2.5 monitoring equipment refers to devices used to measure fine particulate matter in the air, with a diameter of 2.5 micrometers or less. The TEOM monitor, or tapering element oscillating microbalance, utilizes a vibrating filter to measure the mass of particles deposited on it. The Beta Attenuation Monitor uses a beta radiation source to measure particle concentration. Other types of PM2.5 monitors may include optical instruments, laser-based monitors, or gravimetric filters. These monitors play a crucial role in assessing air quality and helping to mitigate the health risks associated with fine particulate matter exposure.</p></p>
