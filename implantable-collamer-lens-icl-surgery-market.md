<p><p>Implantable Collamer Lens (ICL) surgery is a vision correction procedure that involves implanting a collamer lens in the eye to correct refractive errors such as myopia (nearsightedness), hyperopia (farsightedness), and astigmatism. The ICL is made of a biocompatible material called collamer, which is designed to work with the eye's natural physiology.</p><p>During the surgery, a small incision is made, and the ICL is inserted behind the iris and in front of the eye's natural lens. The lens remains hidden and doesn't require any maintenance. It provides excellent visual quality and can be an alternative to LASIK surgery for individuals who are not suitable candidates for laser eye surgery.</p><p>The market for ICL surgery has been witnessing significant growth in recent years. Factors contributing to this growth include an increasing prevalence of refractive errors, growing demand for minimally invasive vision correction procedures, and technological advancements in collamer lens design. Additionally, the rise in disposable income and improving healthcare infrastructure in developing countries are expected to drive market growth.</p><p>The growing prevalence of myopia among the global population is also a major driver for the ICL surgery market. Myopia is projected to affect about half of the world's population by 2050, leading to increased demand for vision correction procedures like ICL surgery.</p><p>The latest trend in the ICL surgery market is the use of advanced technology and techniques, such as the use of femtosecond lasers for precise incision creation and accurate ICL placement. The development of phakic IOLS (intraocular lenses) with advanced features, such as UV protection and blue light filtration, is also gaining traction in the market.</p><p>In conclusion, the Implantable Collamer Lens (ICL) surgery market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period.
