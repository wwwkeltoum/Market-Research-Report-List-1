<p><p>An implantable infusion port, also known as a subcutaneous port, is a small medical device placed under the skin that allows for long-term access to the bloodstream. The port is usually placed in the upper chest or arm, and it is accessed using a needle through the skin.</p><p>The implantable infusion port market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. The rising prevalence of chronic diseases, such as cancer and cardiovascular diseases, is a major factor driving the demand for implantable infusion ports. These devices are commonly used in the administration of chemotherapy drugs, antibiotics, and other medications, improving patient comfort and reducing the risk of infections.</p><p>Additionally, the increasing geriatric population and advancements in medical technology are also contributing to the market growth.
