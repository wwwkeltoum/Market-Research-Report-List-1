<p><strong>The Medical Exoskeletons Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Powered Exoskeletons</li><li>Passive Exoskeletons</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Medical exoskeletons are wearable devices designed to enhance mobility and functionality for individuals with physical disabilities or limitations. Powered exoskeletons use motors, sensors, and actuators to provide assistance and augment the user's strength and movement. They are particularly useful for rehabilitation and enabling paraplegics to walk. On the other hand, passive exoskeletons, also known as mechanical exoskeletons, do not rely on external power and instead use springs, cables, and hinges to provide support and assist with movement. They are lighter, simpler, and less expensive than powered exoskeletons, making them more suitable for everyday use.</p></p>
