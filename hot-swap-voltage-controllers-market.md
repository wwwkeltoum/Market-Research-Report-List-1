<p><p>Hot swap voltage controllers are used to manage power supply and protect electronic circuits during operations such as insertion or removal of circuit cards or hot plug events. High voltage hot swap controllers are designed to operate with high voltage systems, typically over 50V. Low voltage hot swap controllers, on the other hand, are suitable for low voltage applications. PCI hot swap controllers are specifically tailored for the PCI interface, granting safe insertion and removal of PCI cards. These market segments cater to different voltage requirements and specific system interfaces.</p></p>
