Standard treatment options for HER2-negative breast cancer depend on various factors including the stage and characteristics of the tumor, as well as individual patient factors.</p><p>Treatment options for HER2-negative breast cancer may include surgery (lumpectomy or mastectomy), radiation therapy, chemotherapy, hormone therapy, and targeted therapy. Chemotherapy may be recommended before surgery (neoadjuvant chemotherapy) to shrink the tumor or after surgery (adjuvant chemotherapy) to kill any remaining cancer cells. Hormone therapy may be used if the tumor is hormone receptor-positive, while targeted therapies like CDK inhibitors may be used in certain cases.</p><p>The HER2-negative breast cancer treatment market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
