<p><p>Balsamic vinegar is available in two main market types: Traditional Balsamic and Commercial Balsamic. Traditional Balsamic is produced in a specific area of Italy, using traditional methods and aged for a minimum of 12 years. It has a rich and complex flavor, with a thick and syrupy consistency. Commercial Balsamic, on the other hand, is produced using modern methods, often outside of Italy. It is aged for a shorter period and has a less intense flavor. Commercial Balsamic is more widely available and tends to be more affordable compared to Traditional Balsamic.</p></p>
