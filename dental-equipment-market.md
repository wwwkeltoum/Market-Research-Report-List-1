<p><p>Diagnostic dental equipment refers to the tools used by dentists to examine, diagnose, and evaluate the condition of a patient's teeth and gums. This includes X-ray machines, intraoral cameras, dental lasers, and other devices that aid in the detection of dental issues. On the other hand, therapeutic dental equipment refers to the tools used for treating dental problems. This includes dental drills, scalers, dental chairs, and other instruments used for restorative procedures, such as fillings, extractions, and root canals. Together, these two types of equipment assist dentists in providing comprehensive oral care.</p></p>
