<p><p>A vapor chamber is a two-dimensional structure that utilizes a combination of wick structures and liquid-vapor phase change to efficiently dissipate heat from electronic devices. It consists of a sealed chamber filled with a small amount of liquid, usually water or a coolant, which vaporizes and condenses within the chamber, facilitating the transfer of heat away from the heat-generating components of the device. Vapor chambers are widely used in industries such as consumer electronics, automotive, aerospace, and healthcare, where heat management is crucial for optimal performance and reliability of electronic devices.</p><p>The vapor chamber market is experiencing significant growth and is expected to continue expanding at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 14.20% during the forecast period.
