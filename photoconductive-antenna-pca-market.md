<p><p>The Photoconductive Antenna (PCA) market encompasses several types of antennas that operate based on the photoconductive effect. Butterfly Antennas consist of two metallic electrodes connected by a semiconductor strip. Parallel Line Antennas feature two parallel conducting lines with a gap between them. Bow-tie Antennas have a distinctive bow-tie shape, enabling wideband operation. Logarithmic Spiral Antennas use spiral-shaped conducting strips. Finger Gap Antennas have finger-like conducting strips with gaps, enabling high-frequency operation. Other market segments include various types of PCAs designed for specific applications, such as terahertz imaging and spectroscopy.</p></p>
